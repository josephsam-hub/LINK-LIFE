Facts about the blood supply

  • Safe process with sterile needles used once per donor.
  • Four-step process: registration, medical history, mini-physical, donation, and refreshments.
  • Mini-physical checks include temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin.
  • Donation typically takes less than 10-12 minutes, totaling about an hour and 15 minutes.
  • Average adult has about 10 units of blood, with 1 unit donated during donation.
  • Healthy donors can donate red blood cells every 56 days or double red cells every 112 days.

    • Blood Donation Guidelines
    • Age group: 18-60 years.
    • Weight: 45 kgs or more.
    • Haemoglobin: 12.5 gm% minimum.
    • Last blood donation: 3 months prior.
    • Health: No recent history of malaria, typhoid, or transmissible diseases.

    • Blood Donation Restrictions
    • Not donating blood if:
    • Cold/fever in the past week.
    • Under antibiotic or medication treatment.
    • Cardiovascular problems, hypertension, epilepsy, diabetes, history of cancer, chronic kidney or liver disease, bleeding tendencies, venereal disease.
    • Major surgery in the last 6 months.
    • Last 24 hours vaccination.
    • Miscarriage in the last 6 months or pregnancy/lactating in the last year.
    • Fevering attacks during last donation.
    • Regular treatment with blood products.
